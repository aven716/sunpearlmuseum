Fernando Zóbel de Ayala

Fernando Zóbel de Ayala was a renowned Spanish-Filipino painter and art collector born in Manila in 1924. He studied Fine Arts at Harvard University and the Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando in Madrid. Zóbel's artistic style evolved from abstract expressionism to geometric abstraction, characterized by bold colors and innovative techniques.

Historical Background

The painting "España y Filipinas" reflects the historical ties between Spain and the Philippines during a significant period. Created in 1965, it coincided with the final years of Francisco Franco's dictatorship in Spain and a time of post-colonial nation-building in the Philippines.The painting serves as a visual representation of the shared past and enduring connection between Spain and the Philippines
