Tampuhan

by Elmer Borlongan

Elmer Borlongan

Elmer Borlongan is a Filipino Asian Modern & Contemporary artist who was born in 1967. Numerous key galleries and museums such as Galerie Géraldine Banier have featured Elmer Borlongan's work in the past.

Historical Background

The painting depicts a family reunion set in the interiors of Dr. Joven Cuanang's Antipolo home, where Emong, a member of the artist group Salingpusa, draws inspiration. It's an early exploration of the fisheye perspective. The title is taken from Juan Luna’s Tampuhan, a painting displayed in the house. Even the statue of Our Lady of Antipolo is prominently featured. Despite being rushed due to Emong's involvement in the Karnabal mural project commissioned by the CCP, the painting was completed for the Metrobank deadline.

Philosophy

Borlongan might be prompting us to consider the complexities of family relationships, where love and connection coexist with conflict. By incorporating Luna's "Tampuhan" within the painting, it creates a conversation between past and present. Borlongan's detailed portrayal elevates a seemingly ordinary family gathering to a significant event. This suggests a philosophy of finding beauty and meaning in the ordinary aspects of Filipino life.

Characteristics

The scene's interior setting likely features a dominance of warm and earthy tones. This could include browns, beiges, yellows, and possibly some reds. These colors create a sense of intimacy and lived-in quality within the family space. It is created with oil on a canvas that has size of 91 x 122 cm.
