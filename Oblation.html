Guillermo Tolentino

Guillermo Tolentino

Guillermo Estrella Tolentino was born on July 24, 1890, in Malolos, Bulacan, Philippines. He showed an early talent in sculpting, molding figures of horses and dogs out of clay. Tolentino studied at the School of Fine Arts of the University of the Philippines, where he graduated in 1915 with a degree in Fine Arts.

Historical Background

"Oblation" is a sculpture by Guillermo Tolentino portraying a kneeling man offering a bowl, symbolizing sacrifice and selflessness. It embodies the Filipino spirit of patriotism and service, often used to honor national heroes. The sculpture's detailed craftsmanship and powerful stance elevate its significance as an iconic symbol of sacrifice and devotion in Filipino culture.

Philosophy

The sculpture depicts a man facing upward with arms outstretched, symbolizing the selfless offering of oneself to the nation. For Tolentino, the Oblation represents the Filipino youth capable of self-sacrifice in the name of nation-building. Its meaning is associated with sacrifice for the common good and academic freedom.

Characteristics

The Oblation by Guillermo Tolentino is a large-scale sculpture measuring approximately 267.9 x 500.3 x 321.3 cm.. The overall texture of the sculpture is sleek and fluid, enhancing the sense of harmony and balance in the composition. The color palette of the sculpture "Oblation" by Guillermo Tolentino includes shades of bronze, silver, gold, and green.
