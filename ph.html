PRE-COLONIAL

Believed to be the age of the Horticulture/Neolithic period (6185 to 4400 BC),
local communities are being established and art starts to go beyond mere
craft such as stone weapons and jewelry but starts to have decorative elements, meaning, and context.

SPANISH COLONIAL

The Filipinos were introduced to formal painting, sculpture, and architecture which was
inspired by Byzantine, Gothic, Baroque, and Rococo art. Most artworks are religious(Catholic).
Spanish colonialism lived on with the Filipino
“antique” furniture and carving designs. In the formation of the elite Filipino class
who studied abroad had been in contact with the “Western” and had borrowed
Neo-Classicism, Romanticism, and even a hint of impressionism.

AMERICAN COLONIAL

The Americans brought in Education and Value Formation, with both following the
“American way of life”(Allice Guillermo, Sining Biswal,1994,p.4). Art illustration,
advertising, and commercial design gained popularity and were incorporated into Fine Arts.

POST-COLONIAL

This was the art after the War, which grew and expanded.
There were even two periods of art during this time:
[1] the Philippine Modern Art (1946 to 1970), and [2] the Philippine
Post-Modern Art (1970-the1980s). Pop Art, Installation Art,
and Performance Art were
dominating the post-modern period of the Philippine Art
