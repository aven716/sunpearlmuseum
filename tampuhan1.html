Tampuhan

by Juan Luna

Juan Luna

Juan Luna, the renowned Filipino painter, created this masterpiece. Luna was not only an artist but also an active participant in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

Historical Background

Tampuhan portrays a Filipino man and a Filipino woman engaged in a lovers’ quarrel. They are depicted inside the sala or living room of a Bahay na Bato, a traditional colonial Filipino house. The painting captures the emotional tension between the two lovers, who are experiencing “tampo” due to an argument.

Philosophy

Tampuhan reflects aspects of Filipino culture, courtship, and psychology. In Filipino relationships, “tampuhan” (derived from the Filipino word “tampo”) signifies a disagreement between a couple where they stop speaking to each other. It’s akin to “the silent treatment.” Other expressions of sulking include being unusually quiet, refusing to interact with friends or family, or even locking oneself in a room. To resolve the sulking, one lover must coax the other or find a compromise.

Characteristics

"The Tampuhan" is an oil painting, created using oil paints on a canvas surface. The exact dimensions of "The Tampuhan" are approximately 125 cm × 158 cm (49 in × 62 in). The color palette of "The Tampuhan" is dominated by warm, earthy tones, with rich browns, reds, and yellows predominating.
